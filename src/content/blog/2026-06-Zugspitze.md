# Report

Some time ago, I had a vision to climb the Matterhorn (Switzerland). However, I do not have enough
experience to climb it safely. In the past, I was regularly visiting climbing walls, but I never climbed
anything above level 7. Moreover, during the last years in HH, I was mainly cycling and running. I
really miss mountains. I shared my wish with one of my friends (H.), who originates from the Alpine
region, and he suggested that I test myself first on the highest peak in Germany: Zugspitze.

The little expedition started by leaving for Kempten in Bavaria around 10 pm and meeting H.,
who offered me a place to sleep. I went to sleep around 11 pm and got up around 3:30 in the
morning. After a quick breakfast, we traveled down to the ... There we parked the car and started
climbing up to the hut at 6:20. We wanted to reach the Klettersteig sections before they would be
crowded. Also, the weather forecast for the later afternoon ... indicated a possibility of storms at higher
altitudes. I know how fast the weather can change from my home in the middle of the mountains (~1000
m), and therefore I was very much in favor of this plan for a fast climb up and a quick descent down to the
valleys.

The first hour and a half, we were on the pedestrian path leading through tunnels above ... meters.
On the way, there were a few stunning views of the strength of the stream falling from the mountains above
--- just check the photos below. Also, there were Alpine salamanders. H. told me that these are quite
rare. Well, I started counting after approximately the tenth and got to thirty-five (Was it luck? Or are
they not so rare after all? link). Our decision was apparently a good one, as we saw immediately when we got to the hut, where at least another thirty mountainers were preparing for the climb. The closer we got to the via ferrata section, the more people were around us. These surprising numbers were maybe the result of the report about very good conditions ahead of us on the ice.

Finally, we reached the first via Ferrata section. This was rated by a B difficulty, and although
there was maybe a fifty-meter-deep fall below us, it was rather quick to climb through, and we
quickly passed some slower climbers. Then it followed by another few kilometers walk through stone
road up to the 2000 m elevation. Suddenly, I started to feel my zero acclimatization preparation. My legs were significantly heavier than what I would expect concerning the previous climb. I significantly slowed down. At the end of the stoney road we reached last iceberg in Germany (link).
