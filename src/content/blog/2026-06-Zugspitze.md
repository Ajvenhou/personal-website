# Info
- A short report from the successful summit of Zugspitze (2962 m).
- Description of the climbing route with few notes about via Ferrata across Höllental and the way
  back through the Partnach valley
- Final ideas about the whole campaign and important take-home messages.

# Report
Some time ago, I had a vision to climb the Matterhorn (Switzerland). However, I do not have enough
experience to climb it safely. In the past, I was regularly visiting climbing walls, but I never
climbed anything above level 7. Moreover, during the last years in HH, I was mainly cycling and
running. I really miss mountains. I shared my wish with one of my friends (H.), who originates from
the Alpine region, and he suggested that I test myself first on the highest peak in
Germany---Zugspitze.

The little expedition started by leaving for Kempten in Bavaria around 10 pm and meeting H., who
offered me a place to sleep. I went to sleep around 11 pm and got up around 3:30 in the morning.
After a quick breakfast, we rode to the Hammersbach parking lot. There we parked the car and started
climbing up at 6:20 to the Höllentalanger Hut (1400 m). We wanted to reach the first Klettersteig
section before they would be crowded. Also, the weather forecast for the later afternoon, which
indicated a possibility of storms at higher altitudes. I know how quickly weather can change from my
hometown in the middle of the mountains with significantly lower altitudes, and therefore I was very
much in favor of the plan of a fast climb up and a quick descent down to the valleys.

The first hour and a half, we were on the pedestrian path next to a fast water stream leading
through tunnels and stairs. On the way, there were a few stunning views of the strength of the
stream falling from the mountains and few waterfalls above --- just check the photos below. Also,
there were Alpine salamander (*Salamandra atra*). H. told me that these are quite rare. Well, I
started counting after approximately the tenth and got to thirty-five (Was it luck? Or are they not
so rare after all? https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alpine_salamander). Our decision of the fast climb
was apparently a good one, as we saw immediately when we got to the Höllentalanger Hut, there were
at least another thirty mountainers, who were preparing for the climb. The closer we got to the via
ferrata section, the more people were around us. These surprising numbers were maybe the result of
the good report about the good conditions for climbing on the iceberg ahead of us.

Finally, we reached the first via Ferrata section. This was rated by a B difficulty, and although
there was maybe a fifty-meter-deep fall below us, it was rather quick to climb through, and we
quickly passed some slower climbers. Then it followed by another 1-2 kilometers walk through stone
road up to the 2000 m elevation. Suddenly, I started to feel my zero acclimatization preparation. My
legs were significantly heavier than what I would expect concerning the previous climb. I
significantly slowed down. At the end of the stoney road we reached Höllental --- the last glacier
in Germany (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Höllentalferner). *Note: Later I learnt that there is
another glacier in Germany called Schneeferner https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schneeferner.* I was
lucky borrowed me shoespikes, otherwise it would be very slippery uphill. Anyway, I slipped once and
went down by 5 m. Next time I will have an axe with me. At the end of the glacier started the second
section of *via Ferrata*, this time labelled by C mark.

After a short changing of shoespikes, we wanted to start climbing again, however, there was already
a queue of climbers. It took roughly 5 to 10 minutes to get on the wall. It was more difficult than
I assumed. The problem was not a wall but that we had to stay there for a very long time waiting for
mountainers above us to get up. Also my boots were too large for some of the climb sections, so I
couldnt rest. Finally, we got up the steep wall and we started climbing hard, but not as dangerous
part of klettersteig. It took me two to three hours. The peak looked very close all the time, but it
was hard.

Finally, I noticed that I am 50 meters from the summit. H. was already waiting for me and we could
together get to the cross at the top (roughly at 12:50), took a photo and went to the huge tourist
building at the top. There I had a short snack and we started to think what next. I even thought
that maybe a cabin down would not be a so bad idea. Anyway, after one hour rest we felt that we can
walk down. I was definitely against the hard klettersteig, which was later found a critically wrong
decidion. So we decided to take a easy way by via Ferrata marked by A. First 2 kilometers H. was
still faster than me, but when we got below 2500 m my legs started to be again with a normal weigh
and I was again faster.

After roughly 10 kilometers close to Knorr Hut we found out that the current route is indeed
technically much easier. However, we have to go another 20 kilomers, which practically means that we
will have 40 kilometers expedition in quite hard terrain. Well, there was no other option. Luckilly,
we did not have to climb any other mountain, and so we were just walking along the river Partnach to
the town Garmisch-Partenkirchen. I felt suprisingly a lot of energy, however, my hard boots and
almost zero preparation start to taken its tax. At thirtieth kilometer I started to feel blisters
all over both my feets. And the last five kilometers were just pure pain. Thanks god H. was wearing
running shoes which in the end were much better choice for our fast walk. And therefore, when I got
to Garmisch-Partenkirchen, we agreed that he will walk faster to the car and I will walk towards the
train station, where he will deliver me my large backpack fot the way back to HH. All was done as
agreed, we made a farewell and I took the night train to HH at 10 pm.

The travel back was harder for my fellow companions than for me. I tried to clean myself in the
train bathroom, but still---sorry to the lady sitting close to me. Also, the whole Sunday I was
walking like a cowboy until my blisters healed themself.

Overall, this expedition was much more than I expected and I learned far more than I wanted for the
first true mountainering experrience. 

# Quick notes
- Take home experience: Support rope for sitting on the wall. Better check of the routes. One day of
  acclimatization for mountains around 3000 m.
